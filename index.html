Eleanor of Aquitaine

Summary

Eleanor of Aquitaine lived in the 12th Century. She was the Duchess of Aquitaine, the Queen of France and the Queen of England. Her real name was Alienor.

Sections

Early Days

She was born in Aquitaine (now part of France) When her father died, she was the richest heiress in the known world. She had to escape men looking to kidnap her, and force her to marry them, so they would become rich, and a duke of the largest and most prosperous dukedom.

Queen of France

Eventually, the prince of France succeeded in kidnapping her and marrying her(she was 13). When the king and queen of France died, she became queen of France. When she couldn’t bear boy children, the Prince of France annulled their marriage.

Queen of England

Yet again, people tried kidnapping her. Eventually she fell in love with the heir of England, Henry. She had four sons. Eventually, her husband started hating her, and shut her away in prison until she was 62. She was let out when her husband died.

Regent

When Henry died, she led an army against the French to secure her son Richard’s claim to the throne. When her son Richard was taken captive, she raised a ransom of 150,000 marks. When Richard died, she acted as regent until her youngest son was ready to rule.
